# Combat, Loadouts & Survival

How to fight efficiently, survive heists, and avoid losing money/time to griefers. Good combat = fewer failed missions = more $/hr.

## Core loadout

- **Primary weapons:** an assault rifle (Carbine Rifle or **Special Carbine** Mk II) for general combat; a **Heavy Sniper Mk II** for long range / Cayo guards; an **SMG / Micro SMG Mk II** for vehicles/close quarters; a **Combat Shotgun** or **Assault Shotgun** for rooms.
- **Mk II ammo (huge):** **Explosive rounds** on the Heavy Sniper (one-shot most things, blow up vehicles), **Incendiary/Hollow-point** on the Special Carbine. Buy Mk II ammo cheap from the **Gun Van** daily discount. The MkII upgrades come from the **Bunker (Research)**.
- **Pistol:** **Up-n-Atomizer** (free push gun, flips/clears vehicles off you) and the **Heavy Revolver Mk II** for utility.
- **Suppressor** on at least one weapon for **stealth** (Cayo, Casino Silent & Sneaky).
- **Throwables:** Sticky Bombs (objectives, griefers), Proximity Mines (defend sell vehicles).

## Armor, snacks & utility (survivability)

- Always carry **Super Heavy Armor** (max body armor) and restock at the Ammu-Nation/Interaction Menu before missions.
- **Snacks** heal you mid-fight from the Interaction Menu — stock **up to max** (chips/eCola/Sprunk + the orange/purple snacks). They're the difference between surviving a heist firefight and dying.
- Carry a few **Armor + Snacks** before every heist finale; re-stock between runs.
- **Bull Shark Testosterone / Boost** optional.
- **Heavy Revolver / Bodyarmor + snack management** is what keeps you alive on Cayo and Casino aggressive.

## Vehicles for combat & grinding

- **Cayo / general:** an **armored Kosatka Sparrow** (or **Buzzard**) — fast helicopter with miniguns/missiles for prep skips and quick travel. The **Sparrow** lands on/launches from the Kosatka.
- **Anti-griefer / defense:** **Oppressor Mk II** (mobility + missiles, the lobby staple), or for budget the **Toreador** (submersible, missiles, defensive) or **Vigilante**.
- **Sell-mission defense:** keep a **Toreador / armored vehicle** nearby when selling in public.
- Don't bring fragile vehicles to combat content; use armored/weaponized ones.

## Fighting NPCs (heists, missions)

- **Headshots** end most NPCs instantly — aim high, use a scope on snipers.
- Use **cover**; don't run into open ground. Peek-and-shoot.
- For **stealth** (Cayo, Casino S&S): use a **suppressed weapon**, take guards from behind, avoid cameras/spotlights, and don't sprint near guards (noise). Mark guards with the heist camera/binoculars beforehand.
- **Explosive sniper rounds** trivialize tough enemy vehicles and groups.

## Fighting / surviving other players (anti-grief)

- **The #1 anti-grief move: don't sell in a hostile public lobby.** Use an **invite-only**, **friends-only**, or **solo public** session for sales and setups. You forgo the high-demand bonus but guarantee delivery. (How-to: pause menu → Online → New Session → Invite Only Session.)
- If you must be in public: enable **Passive Mode** (Interaction Menu) when not in a sell/mission — you can't be shot by other players' bullets (still vulnerable to vehicles/explosives).
- **Ghost Organization** (CEO/MC ability) hides you from the radar of distant players for a couple of minutes.
- Against an **Oppressor Mk II** griefer: use the **Up-n-Atomizer** or **Explosive sniper**, get indoors/under cover, switch to a passive/solo session, or call **Lester's "Off the Radar."**
- Keep **Lester's "Off the Radar"** and **Ghost Org** on cooldown-ready during risky sells.
- Don't engage griefers for revenge during a grind — it wastes time. Leave to a fresh/solo session and keep earning.

## Death/time costs to avoid

- Dying loses time and a small hospital fee, and **failing a sell mission can lose the whole batch** — defense matters more than offense when selling.
- Don't drive sell vehicles recklessly (some are fragile and explode). Take the safe route.
- Re-stock **armor + snacks** between every heist; entering a finale under-equipped risks the whole payout.
